## Week 3-4: Post-Launch Momentum (Days 8-21)

### **Monday (Day 8)**

**Platform:** Twitter  
**Content Type:** Educational thread  
**Post:**

```
How to find a technical co-founder in 2026 (without LinkedIn):

A thread 👇
```

**Thread:**

```
1/ Stop cold messaging on LinkedIn.

Response rate: 2-5%
Time wasted: Weeks
Success rate: Near zero

There's a better way.

2/ Go where developers actually hang out:

✅ GitHub (contribute to open source)
✅ Indie Hackers (join discussions)
✅ Dev.to (comment on articles)
✅ Local meetups (in-person > online)
✅ Hackathons (see them in action)

3/ Don't lead with your idea.

Lead with value.

Offer to:
• Beta test their project
• Introduce them to potential users
• Give feedback on their code
• Share your marketing skills

Build a relationship first.

4/ When you DO pitch your idea:

❌ "I need a technical co-founder to build my app"
✅ "I've validated this problem with 50 users and have $10k in pre-orders. Want to build it together?"

Show traction, not just an idea.

5/ Use tools built for this:

StartupVerse has Smart Team Matching (free)
YC Co-Founder Matching (free)
CoFoundersLab ($30/mo)

These have higher quality matches than LinkedIn.

6/ Red flags to watch for:

🚩 They want equity but no risk
🚩 They've never finished a project
🚩 They won't sign a co-founder agreement
🚩 They disappear for weeks
🚩 They want to be CEO (not CTO)

7/ Green flags:

✅ They've shipped products before
✅ They ask tough questions about the business
✅ They commit to 20+ hours/week
✅ They're excited about the problem, not just tech
✅ They suggest improvements to your idea

8/ Final tip:

The best co-founder is someone you've worked with before.

If you don't have that, work on a small project together first (1-2 weeks).

See if you're compatible before committing.

9/ And if you're looking right now:

Try StartupVerse's Smart Team Matching: [link]

We've helped 50+ founders find co-founders in the last week.

Good luck! 🚀
```

## Engagement Guidelines

### **Response Time:**

- First 100 followers: < 1 hour
- 100-1,000 followers: < 2 hours
- 1,000-10,000 followers: < 4 hours
- 10,000+ followers: < 24 hours

### **Engagement Ratio:**

For every 1 post you make, engage with 3-5 other posts:

- Comment on founder posts
- Answer questions in communities
- Share other people's wins
- Provide value without asking for anything

### **Build Relationships:**

- Follow 10-20 founders per week
- DM 5 people per week (genuine, not salesy)
- Join Twitter Spaces (listen > talk)
- Collaborate with complementary products
